About Washburn University

Washburn University is a public institution located in Topeka, Kansas. It offers undergraduate and graduate programs through its various schools and colleges, including the College of Arts and Sciences, School of Business, School of Law, and School of Nursing.

Founded in 1865, Washburn University has a rich history of providing high-quality education and fostering a supportive community for students.

Academics

Washburn University offers a wide range of academic programs, including:

  • Bachelor's degrees
  • Master's degrees
  • Doctoral degrees
  • Professional programs

Students have access to state-of-the-art facilities and resources to support their learning and research endeavors.

Admissions

Washburn University welcomes students from diverse backgrounds who are passionate about pursuing higher education. The admissions process includes:

  • Application submission
  • Transcript submission
  • Standardized test scores (optional)
  • Letters of recommendation

Prospective students can visit the admissions office or explore the university's website for more information.

Student Life

At Washburn University, students have the opportunity to engage in a vibrant campus life. Whether through student organizations, athletics, or cultural events, there's something for everyone.

From participating in intramural sports to attending concerts and performances, students can enrich their college experience and make lasting memories.
